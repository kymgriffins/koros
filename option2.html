01 — Manifesto · The Problem

Kenya's Budget Is Public. But Is It Accessible?

Budget documents are legally required to be published. The National Treasury does publish them. The 47 county governments publish theirs too. But they are written in procurement code and bureaucratic shorthand, filed in multi-hundred-page PDFs that require a finance degree to navigate. Budget Ndio Story exists to change that permanently.

01 — Manifesto · The Stakes

When Citizens Can't Read the Budget, Nobody Wins.

A constituency development project that was never funded. A hospital promised in a governor's speech that never appears in the county budget. A youth programme running a year behind because nobody knew it was underfunded. These aren't hypotheticals — they happen every year across all 47 counties. Budget literacy is the missing layer of Kenya's democracy.

02 — Method · Our Approach

Investigate. Translate. Activate.

We obtain every budget document from official government sources, cross-reference each figure with at least two independent records, and then translate the findings into visual reports, short videos, and interactive tools — all built for citizens, not bureaucrats. Then we build the civic infrastructure to put that knowledge to work: forums, trackers, and Budget Champions in all 47 counties.
