Budget Ndio Story — Kenya's Budget, Decoded for Every Citizen

Every year, Kenya's government spends over KSh 4 trillion across hundreds of programmes. All 47 counties control additional billions in devolved funds. But government budget documents are written for bureaucrats, not citizens. Budget Ndio Story changes that — translating Kenya's entire public finance system into clear, visual, accessible information. Free to every Kenyan. Forever.

01 — Manifesto

Why We
Exist

Kenya's budget is technically public. But technical and accessible are not the same thing. We close that gap — permanently, and for free.

01
The Transparency Gap
Kenya's national budget exceeds KSh 4 trillion and is published by law. County governments publish theirs too. But these documents are written to comply with regulations — not to communicate with citizens. Dense tables, technical jargon, and 300-page PDFs create a wall between Kenyans and the decisions that shape their lives. Budget Ndio Story tears down that wall.
02
The Democratic Cost
When citizens cannot read the budget, they cannot hold power accountable. They cannot verify whether the hospital in their ward was actually funded. They cannot attend a county budget forum with the right questions. Budget literacy is the missing foundation of Kenya's democracy — and right now it belongs to too few people.
03
Our Response
Budget Ndio Story is a public good, not a product. We read every budget document — national, county, sector — so you don't have to. Then we translate the findings into visual reports, explainers, and tools built for the next generation of Kenyan citizens. Every piece of content we produce is free to access, forever.
04
For Every Kenyan
Whether you're a student in Kisii, a journalist in Nairobi, an activist in Mombasa, or a ward rep in Garissa — this platform is built for you. Kenya's budget story belongs to every citizen who pays taxes, uses a public school, or visits a government hospital. That's all 55 million of us.
"

The budget is a moral document. If you want to know what your government values — read its balance sheet.

— Budget Ndio Story Manifesto, 2026

02 — Method · Step One

Investigation: We Read Everything

Every budget document published by the National Treasury, Controller of Budget, Auditor General, and all 47 county governments is obtained, catalogued, and cross-referenced. Our team verifies every figure against at least two independent sources before publication. No unverified number ever reaches our platform.

02 — Method · Step Two

Translation: Numbers Become Narratives

Raw data is meaningless without context. We pair every figure with the story behind it — who decided it, what changed year-on-year, and what it means in practice for citizens. We produce visual reports, interactive charts, short-form videos, and social content designed to reach Kenyans where they are: on phones, in classrooms, in communities.
