01 — Manifesto

Why Budget Ndio Story Exists

Kenya's public finance data is technically public — but practically inaccessible. We exist to close that gap, permanently.

01
01.A

The Transparency Problem

Kenya's budget documents are published by law — but they are written for compliance, not communication. Dense tables, acronyms, and 300-page PDFs create a wall between citizens and the decisions that shape their lives. Budget Ndio Story tears down that wall, translating every document into language that every Kenyan can understand and act on.

AccountabilityAccessClarity
01.B

The Democratic Stakes

When citizens cannot read the budget, they cannot meaningfully participate in public forums. They cannot question an MCA about ward project allocations. They cannot verify whether a hospital promised in a county speech was ever funded. Budget literacy is the foundation of democratic accountability — and right now, it belongs to too few people.

DemocracyParticipation
01.C

Our Commitment

Budget Ndio Story is a public good, not a product. Every report, every dataset, every explainer we produce will be free to access, forever. We believe that transparency infrastructure should be owned by everyone — and that the next generation of Kenyans deserves the tools to hold power accountable without a paywall in the way.

Free ForeverOpen Data
01.D

For Every Kenyan

Whether you're a secondary school student in Kisii, a journalist in Nairobi, a community organiser in Mombasa, or a county assembly member in Garissa — this platform is built for you. Kenya's budget story belongs to every citizen who pays taxes, uses a school, or needs a hospital. That's all of us.

02 — Method

How We Do It

Three rigorous steps from raw government data to informed citizen action. No shortcuts. No compromises.

02
02.A — Investigation

We Read Everything So You Don't Have To

Every budget document published by the National Treasury, Controller of Budget, Auditor General, and all 47 county governments is obtained, catalogued, and cross-referenced. Our team validates every number against at least two independent sources before any figure is published on this platform. No unverified data ever reaches our audience.

OCDS DataIFMIS RecordsCoB ReportsAudit Trails
02.B — Translation

Numbers Become Narratives

Raw data is meaningless without context. We pair every figure with the story behind it — who decided it, what it means in practice, and what changed year on year. We produce visual reports, interactive charts, short-form video explainers, and social content designed to reach Kenyans where they already are: on their phones, in their communities, in their classrooms.

Visual ReportsVideoInfographics
02.C — Activation

Information That Creates Action

Transparency without participation is incomplete. That's why we build practical civic tools alongside our content — templates for county budget forums, ward-level spending trackers, and a training programme for Budget Champions: one trained community representative per county to lead local accountability efforts across Kenya's 47 devolved units.
