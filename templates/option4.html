01 — Manifesto

Kenya's Budget Is Published. But Who Can Read It?

Kenya's national budget exceeds KSh 4 trillion annually. County governments control another KSh 500 billion in devolved funds. By law, all of this must be publicly available. And it is — buried in multi-hundred-page PDFs, written in procurement codes and technical jargon, filed under obscure acronyms on government portals that are difficult to navigate even for specialists.

Budget Ndio Story was created because transparency on paper is not the same as transparency in practice. We exist to close the gap between what government publishes and what citizens can actually understand and use.

02 — The Stakes

What Happens When Citizens Can't Follow the Money?

When citizens cannot read the budget, a cascade of accountability failures becomes possible. A ward development project can be allocated on paper and disappear on delivery. A healthcare programme can be underfunded for years before anyone outside Treasury notices. A governor can promise an infrastructure project in a speech that never appears in the county's appropriation bill.

These aren't edge cases — they are documented, recurring patterns across Kenya's devolved governance system. Budget literacy is the missing layer of accountability that Budget Ndio Story is built to provide.

03 — Method: Investigation

We Read Every Document. So You Don't Have To.

Budget Ndio Story obtains budget documents directly from primary government sources — the National Treasury IFMIS portal, the Controller of Budget quarterly reports, the Auditor General's annual reports, and the official portals of all 47 county governments. Every figure we publish is cross-referenced against at least two independent sources.

We track Budget Policy Statements, Appropriation Bills, Supplementary Budgets, County Fiscal Strategy Papers, Ward Development Fund allocations, Procurement Plans, and Audit Findings — the complete documentary record of how Kenya's public money moves.

04 — Method: Translation

Numbers Become Narratives. Spreadsheets Become Stories.

Data without context is noise. We produce visual budget summaries, sector-by-sector breakdowns, short-form video explainers, and interactive county dashboards — all designed to work on a phone screen without a finance degree. Our content meets Kenyans where they are: on WhatsApp, on YouTube, on Instagram, in classrooms, and in community halls.

Every piece of content we produce is free to access, share, and use. Budget Ndio Story is a public good, not a product. The information belongs to every citizen it was created for.

05 — Method: Activation

Information Without Action Is Just Trivia.

The final step in our method is turning budget knowledge into citizen power. We build practical civic infrastructure: public participation forum templates for county budget cycles, ward-level expenditure tracker dashboards, question banks for MCA accountability sessions, and freedom of information request templates.

Our flagship civic programme — the Budget Champions initiative — trains one community representative per county to lead local accountability efforts. 47 counties. 47 champions. One platform. The most comprehensive budget accountability programme in Kenya's history.
