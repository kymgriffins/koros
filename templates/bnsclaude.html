01 — Manifesto

Why We
Exist

Kenya's national budget exceeds KSh 4 trillion annually. County governments control another KSh 500 billion. Yet for most young citizens, these numbers remain abstract — trapped in PDFs, buried in jargon, disconnected from daily life.

That ends here.


The Problem

Budget documents are public by law. But they're written for bureaucrats, not citizens. Dense tables, obscure codes, 300-page PDFs — designed to comply, not to communicate.

The Stakes

When citizens can't read the budget, they can't ask the right questions. They can't hold their MCA accountable. They can't know if their ward's road project was ever funded.

Our Answer

We read every budget document so you don't have to. We turn raw data into clarity — visual reports, explainers, and tools built for the next generation of Kenyan citizens.

For Everyone

Free, forever. Whether you're a student, journalist, activist, or just a curious citizen — this platform is yours. Transparency should never be paywalled.

The budget is a
moral document.
Read it that way.
